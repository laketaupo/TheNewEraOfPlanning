Overview

The operational planner lives in the short-term horizon — typically the next 1–4 weeks. Their week is shaped by a recurring rhythm of monitoring, exception resolution, and actuals capture. No two days are identical, but the underlying structure is predictable: check what has changed overnight, resolve what needs resolving, and close out what was promised.

Monday — Set the Week

Morning: Execution Handover Review

  • Pull overnight actuals from ERP — goods receipts, shipments, production confirmations
  • Compare against the week's committed plan; flag any variances above tolerance
  • Reprioritise open orders where supply or capacity has shifted

Afternoon: Exception Triage

  • Open exception queue in planning software; review auto-generated alerts
  • Classify exceptions by severity and time-to-impact
  • Resolve what can be resolved immediately; escalate what cannot

Key systems: ERP (actuals, order management), Planning Software (exception queue, near-term plan)

Tuesday — Supply Monitoring

Morning: Supply Signal Check

  • Review confirmed supply receipts vs. planned receipts
  • Identify at-risk orders (late supplier confirmations, production delays, logistics holds)
  • Update the near-term supply plan in planning software where actuals deviate

Afternoon: Collaboration

  • Sync with logistics coordinator on open shipments
  • Flag procurement on any purchase orders at risk of missing the week's commitment
  • Update exception log with resolution status

Key systems: ERP (purchase orders, production orders), Planning Software (supply monitoring view)

Wednesday — Demand Monitoring

Morning: Demand Signal Refresh

  • Review incoming customer orders and compare against the committed near-term demand plan
  • Identify demand spikes or cancellations that require supply re-allocation
  • Check inventory coverage for the highest-priority SKUs

Afternoon: Allocation and Prioritisation

  • Apply allocation rules to constrained SKUs (per planning policy)
  • Communicate priorities to logistics and warehouse for picking and dispatch sequence

Key systems: ERP (sales orders, inventory), Planning Software (demand monitoring view, allocation logic)

Thursday — S&OE Integrated Review Preparation

Morning: Data Pack Assembly

  • Pull the week's exception summary — what was flagged, what was resolved, what is open
  • Prepare the near-term KPI snapshot: on-time delivery trend, fill rate, open exception count
  • Identify any items needing cross-functional decision at the S&OE review

Afternoon: Integrated S&OE Review

  • Present exception status and resolution actions
  • Escalate unresolved exceptions that require commercial or supply decisions beyond the operational planner's mandate
  • Agree actions and owners before close of day

Key systems: Planning Software (integrated S&OE dashboard), ERP (execution KPIs)

Friday — Close and Capture

Morning: Actuals Capture

  • Confirm all goods receipts, production confirmations, and shipments are posted in ERP
  • Reconcile any discrepancies between system records and warehouse actuals
  • Post corrections where needed so the record is clean for the next planning run

Afternoon: Feedback to Planning

  • Send a brief summary of the week's execution performance to the tactical planner — what moved as planned, what did not, and why
  • Flag any structural issues (recurring exceptions, data quality problems, supplier patterns) that should be raised in the next S&OP cycle

Key systems: ERP (actuals, inventory), Planning Software (feedback mechanism or notes)
