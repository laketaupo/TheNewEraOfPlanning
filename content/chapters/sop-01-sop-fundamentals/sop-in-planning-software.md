## What planning software adds to S&OP

A spreadsheet-based S&OP is slow to prepare, hard to trust, and difficult to update when assumptions change. Planning software replaces the manual assembly of demand, supply, and inventory data with a live planning model — one that updates automatically as inputs change and lets planners run scenarios in minutes rather than days.

## The demand review in planning software

Before the demand review, planners use the system to:

- Run the **statistical baseline forecast** using the configured demand sensing model
- Load **commercial overrides** from sales and marketing into a structured input layer
- Compare the adjusted forecast to the prior month and to budget
- Produce **exception reports** — items where the forecast has changed significantly and needs explanation

During the review, participants view dashboards that show forecast versus actuals, bias trends, and coverage by SKU or product family. Adjustments are made directly in the system, not in a spreadsheet that gets emailed around.

## The supply review in planning software

Planning software runs a **constrained supply plan** against the consensus demand: it checks available capacity, supplier lead times, and current inventory, then projects where shortfalls and surpluses will occur. The supply team reviews a pre-calculated gap report — not a manually assembled one — which makes the review faster and the gaps harder to dispute.

## Scenario analysis for pre-S&OP

Pre-S&OP requires trade-off analysis: what happens if we accept a 5% service level miss versus building 3 weeks of forward inventory? Planning software lets planners create and compare scenarios against a baseline plan, with the financial impact calculated automatically. These scenarios go into the executive review as structured options, not anecdotal estimates.

## Publishing the agreed plan

Once the executive decision is made, the planning software publishes the agreed plan: inventory targets are updated, procurement orders are confirmed, and the S&OE team has a validated baseline to manage against for the next four weeks. The plan becomes the operational authority — not a document in a shared drive.
