What the integrated S&OE review is

The integrated S&OE review is a weekly cross-functional meeting in which the demand, supply, and operations functions align on the near-term plan. It is the decision-making forum for S&OE — the point where the monitoring work done by individual planners during the week is synthesised into collective decisions about what to do.

Without this meeting, S&OE becomes a collection of individual monitoring activities with no shared decision-making mechanism. Individual planners see their own slice of the picture. The integrated review brings those slices together, surfaces conflicts between demand priorities and supply constraints, and produces agreed decisions on how to resolve them.

How it differs from S&OP

The integrated S&OE review and the S&OP executive review serve different purposes, operate at different timescales, and involve different levels of the organisation.

S&OP is a monthly process. It sets the plan for the next 12 to 18 months at the product family level, balancing aggregate demand and supply and reconciling with the financial plan. Its decisions are strategic and structural: capacity investments, sourcing strategies, inventory policy, new product launches.

The integrated S&OE review is a weekly process. It manages the execution of the plan that S&OP set, within the near-term horizon of the next four to eight weeks. It operates at the SKU and order level, not the family level. Its decisions are operational: which orders to prioritise, which exceptions to resolve this week, which supply constraints to work around. It does not revise the strategic plan — it executes it, and escalates when execution is no longer possible within the current plan.

This distinction matters in practice. The S&OE review should not attempt to re-plan what S&OP agreed. Its authority is limited to the near-term window and to actions that can be taken within existing capacity and supply commitments. When a decision requires stepping outside those constraints, it escalates to S&OP.

Granularity: SKU and order level, not family level

One of the clearest structural differences between S&OE and S&OP is the level of granularity at which decisions are made. S&OP works at the product family level because monthly planning cycles cannot be practically managed at the SKU level across thousands of items. The S&OE review works at the SKU and order level precisely because near-term execution decisions are inherently specific — which customer order gets prioritised, which SKU gets the available stock, which PO gets expedited.

This means the S&OE review is dealing with concrete, named situations: a specific late purchase order from a specific supplier, a specific customer order at risk, a specific production sequence decision. The conversations are operational and specific, not strategic and aggregate.

Participants

The integrated S&OE review is a small, focused meeting. The core participants are:

  • S&OE planner — chairs the meeting, presents the integrated monitoring picture, and leads the decision list.
  • Demand planner — presents the demand monitoring summary and any near-term forecast deviations.
  • Supply planner — presents the supply monitoring summary, including PO status, production output, and inventory position.
  • Operations representative — provides visibility on production schedule adherence, capacity constraints, and execution issues that are not yet visible in the planning system.

Depending on the organisation, a procurement representative may join when supplier issues are on the agenda. Senior leadership does not typically attend unless an escalation item requires executive authority — keeping the meeting at the operational level is what allows it to run efficiently week after week.
