## The standard agenda

A well-run integrated S&OE review follows a consistent five-part agenda. Consistency matters: when participants know the structure of the meeting, preparation is faster, discussion stays focused, and decisions are made efficiently. A review that reinvents its own format each week drifts into open-ended discussion and loses its operational discipline.

The five agenda items are: (1) open exceptions from the previous week, (2) demand monitoring summary, (3) supply monitoring summary, (4) decision list, and (5) escalations to S&OP.

## 1. Open exceptions from the previous week (10 minutes)

The first agenda item reviews exceptions that were identified in the previous week's review and have not yet been resolved. Each open exception is checked for status: has the issue been resolved, is it in progress with a clear expected resolution date, or has it not moved?

Exceptions that have not moved without a clear reason become the first escalation candidates. This opening item also serves as accountability tracking — it makes visible whether agreed actions from the previous week were actually taken.

## 2. Demand monitoring summary (10 minutes)

The demand planner presents a brief summary of the demand picture since the last review. This is not a full readout of every exception — the monitoring work has already been done during the week. The summary covers: which demand deviations are material, whether near-term demand is running above or below plan, any customer order patterns that require operational attention, and any exceptions that were resolved during the week without needing the review.

The audience for this section is the supply planner and the operations representative, who need to understand the demand picture to contextualise the supply decisions that follow.

## 3. Supply monitoring summary (10 minutes)

The supply planner presents the supply picture: PO status for the near-term window, production output vs. schedule for the previous week, current inventory position by major SKU or risk category, and active quality holds. Exceptions already resolved during the week are noted briefly. Exceptions that remain open are flagged for the decision list.

The demand planner needs this section to understand whether the demand deviations identified in the previous section can be absorbed by the supply picture, or whether they create an unresolvable conflict that requires a joint decision.

## 4. Decision list (25 minutes)

The decision list is the core of the meeting. It contains all items that require a cross-functional decision before the next review cycle. Items on the decision list typically arise from the monitoring week and from the demand and supply summaries presented in the meeting.

For each decision item, the S&OE planner briefly states the situation, the options available, and the recommended course of action. The meeting then confirms, modifies, or rejects the recommendation. Each decision is recorded with the specific action, the owner, and the expected completion date.

The decision list should be prepared in advance by the S&OE planner, not built during the meeting. Time spent in the room should be on deciding, not on building the list.

## 5. Escalations to S&OP (5 minutes)

The final agenda item reviews any items that the meeting has identified as exceeding the S&OE resolution threshold — situations where the near-term plan cannot be executed within current constraints and a structural plan revision is needed. Each escalation is framed with the same structure: what the issue is, why it cannot be resolved within S&OE, and what decision or input is needed from S&OP.

Escalation items are logged in the planning system and communicated to the S&OP process owner, either as agenda items for the next monthly cycle or, in urgent cases, as requests for an out-of-cycle review.

## Keeping the meeting to 60 minutes

The integrated S&OE review can be run in 60 minutes if the preparation is done. The time allocations above total 60 minutes and assume that monitoring summaries are prepared in advance, exceptions are pre-classified by severity, and the decision list is drafted before the meeting begins. The role of the S&OE planner as chair is to keep discussion focused on the decision at hand and to parking-lot items that require deeper analysis offline.

A review that consistently runs longer than 60 minutes is usually a sign that preparation is incomplete, decision authority is unclear, or the meeting is being used as a forum for analysis rather than for decisions. Each of these is a process problem with a specific remedy.
