## The execution layer

Execution is the operational layer where plans become physical actions: purchase orders are placed with suppliers, production batches are started, shipments are dispatched. In the operating model, execution operates at the shortest time horizon — days and shifts rather than weeks and months.

Execution does not decide what to do. That was determined in S&OP and refined in S&OE. Execution's job is to deliver the confirmed plan accurately and feed outcomes back so the planning model stays current.

## The planner's role in execution

Planners do not execute — they enable execution. The planner's responsibility is to ensure that the plan in Planning software is current and credible, that planned orders are released to ERP on time, and that execution deviations are surfaced and reconciled promptly in S&OE. Where execution is structurally unable to follow the plan, the plan is revised and the deviation is escalated.
