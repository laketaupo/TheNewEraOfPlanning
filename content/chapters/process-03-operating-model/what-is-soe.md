## S&OE: the bridge between planning and execution

Sales & Operations Execution (S&OE) is the weekly cadence that sits between the monthly S&OP plan and day-to-day operational execution. Where S&OP sets the plan for the next 3–18 months, S&OE manages the next 1–6 weeks — the horizon where the plan meets reality.

S&OE is the process where planners answer: "Given what we agreed last month, what's going wrong this week, and what do we do about it?" Its output is not a new plan — it is **corrective action** within the bounds of the existing S&OP plan.

## How S&OP and S&OE relate

The two processes are complementary, not competing. They operate on different time horizons at different levels of detail:

| | S&OP | S&OE |
|---|---|---|
| **Horizon** | 3–18 months | 1–6 weeks |
| **Granularity** | Product family / month | SKU / week |
| **Cadence** | Monthly | Weekly |
| **Purpose** | Align demand, supply, finance | Resolve near-term exceptions |
| **Output** | Agreed plan | Corrective actions |

S&OP without S&OE creates plans that drift from reality within days. S&OE without S&OP creates reactive firefighting without direction.
