The core purpose of S&OP

Sales & Operations Planning (S&OP) is a structured monthly process that brings together demand, supply, inventory, and financial plans into one agreed-upon view of the business. Its purpose is alignment: ensuring that sales commitments are backed by supply capacity, that financial targets are grounded in operational reality, and that cross-functional decisions are made together rather than in silos.

S&OP operates at an aggregate level — typically by product family, region, and month. It is not a day-to-day execution process. It sets the constraints and priorities within which weekly S&OE and daily execution happen.

What S&OP is not

S&OP is not a forecasting meeting. The forecast is an input, not the output. S&OP is also not an operations review — it does not manage last week's shipments or resolve today's quality issue. Those belong in S&OE.

Confusing the two is a common failure mode: executive meetings get dragged into day-to-day firefighting, and the long-term plan never gets the structured attention it needs.
