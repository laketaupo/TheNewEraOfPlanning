## What promotion means

Promotion replaces the current baseline plan with the scenario plan. After promotion, the scenario's planned orders, inventory projections, and exception flags become the authoritative plan that the organisation executes against. This is the point where the simulation becomes operational.

Promotion is irreversible in the sense that the baseline changes — although the prior baseline can be archived and the scenario retained as a reference version.

## When to promote

A simulation should only be promoted when:

1. **The scenario assumption has been confirmed** — the demand signal has been received, the supply constraint has been validated, or the business decision has been made. Do not promote a scenario based on a hypothesis that hasn't been confirmed.

2. **The output has been reviewed and agreed** — the planned orders generated by the scenario have been checked for correctness, and the team is comfortable that the new plan is achievable.

3. **Sign-off has been obtained** — per the scenario governance policy, promotion requires the appropriate level of authorisation (planning manager, or S&OP sign-off for large changes to the baseline).

4. **Execution systems are ready** — if the new plan will generate purchase orders or production orders in ERP, the relevant execution team needs to be prepared for the new planned quantities and timing.

## The promotion process

In Planning software, promotion is typically performed by a planning administrator or planning manager. The steps are:

1. Archive the current baseline (preserve a snapshot for reference)
2. Promote the scenario to baseline
3. Trigger a fresh planning run to confirm the promoted plan calculates correctly
4. Publish the new baseline to the relevant stakeholders
5. Update ERP (ERP) with the new planned orders where the integration requires manual release

## After promotion

Monitor the promoted plan in the first S&OE cycle after promotion. Promotion is the beginning of execution, not the end of the planning process. If actual demand or supply deviates from the promoted plan within the first week or two, the scenario may need to be revised or a new scenario built to reflect the updated reality.
