Overview

The strategic planner operates on the longest planning horizon — typically 12–18 months out, with the monthly S&OP cycle as the primary drumbeat. A quarter contains three full S&OP cycles. Each month, the strategic planner runs or coordinates the full S&OP process: demand review, supply review, inventory review, resource review, and executive S&OP. The quarter-level lens adds a layer above that: reviewing whether the rolling plan is converging on the business target, identifying structural risks that span beyond the monthly horizon, and feeding into any quarterly business review or IBP refresh.

Month 1 of the Quarter — Establish the Baseline

S&OP Cycle 1

Demand Review

  • Receive the statistical baseline from planning software; apply commercial overlays from the sales and category teams
  • Run the consensus demand review: challenge the forecast, agree adjustments, lock the demand plan
  • Document assumptions — what is driving the forecast, what could change it

Supply Review

  • Run the constrained supply plan in planning software against the consensus demand
  • Identify capacity gaps, supplier constraints, and inventory coverage risks
  • Prepare the supply gap view for the pre-S&OP meeting

Pre-S&OP

  • Present aligned demand vs. constrained supply plan
  • Surface unresolved gaps and proposed solutions
  • Gain cross-functional alignment before the executive review

Executive S&OP

  • Present the integrated plan: demand, supply, inventory, and financial view
  • Gain executive decisions on any unresolved trade-offs (volume vs. service vs. cost)
  • Close the monthly plan and publish to planning software

Quarter-level Activities

  • Confirm the quarterly planning calendar with all stakeholders
  • Set the quarter's planning horizon assumptions (market conditions, exchange rates, seasonality)

Key systems: Planning Software (statistical baseline, constrained run, plan publication), ERP (actuals for prior period close), Financial planning tool (P&L reconciliation)

Month 2 of the Quarter — Manage and Refine

S&OP Cycle 2

  • Run the full S&OP cycle again (demand → supply → pre-S&OP → executive S&OP)
  • By Month 2, the baseline from Month 1 provides a comparator: has the plan moved? Why?
  • Focus the demand review on understanding forecast accuracy from Month 1 — where were the variances, and what does that mean for the rolling horizon?
  • Focus the supply review on any structural constraints that emerged during Month 1 execution

Scenario Planning Activities

  • Identify 1–2 business risks or opportunities worth running as scenarios (demand upside, supply disruption, new product launch uncertainty)
  • Build and compare scenarios in planning software; prepare the scenario pack for the pre-S&OP
  • Present the recommended scenario to the executive S&OP for decision

Key systems: Planning Software (scenario builder, comparison view, plan promotion), ERP (actuals)

Month 3 of the Quarter — Close and Look Forward

S&OP Cycle 3

  • Run the full S&OP cycle for the third time
  • By Month 3, the focus shifts: how is the quarter tracking against the original plan? What does that mean for the next quarter?
  • The demand review should include a preliminary view of the next quarter's demand signal
  • The supply review should flag any capacity investments or supplier commitments needed for Q+1 that require decision now

Quarterly Business Review / IBP Input

  • Assemble the quarter's S&OP performance summary:
    • Forecast accuracy trend across the three months
    • Plan adherence: supply side and demand side
    • Inventory coverage vs. target at quarter end
    • Open risks carried into the next quarter
  • Present to the QBR or IBP forum as the planning function's contribution to the quarterly business view
  • Use the QBR outcomes to shape assumptions and priorities for the next quarter's S&OP cycles

Key systems: Planning Software (rolling plan, scenario archive), ERP (quarter-end actuals), Reporting tool (QBR pack)

How the Strategic Planner Connects to the Tactical and Operational Layers

The strategic planner sets the plan that the tactical and operational planners execute against. The dependency runs both ways:

  • Downward: The published monthly plan (from S&OP) is disaggregated by the tactical planner into weekly buckets and executed by the operational planner daily.
  • Upward: The actuals, exception logs, and near-term risk flags produced by the tactical planner feed directly into the strategic planner's demand and supply reviews each month.

A strategic planner who is disconnected from near-term reality builds plans that are structurally unexecutable. The quarterly rhythm only holds together when all three layers are exchanging information on a consistent cadence.
