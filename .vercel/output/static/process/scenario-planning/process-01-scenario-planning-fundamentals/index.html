Chapter 1

Scenario Planning Fundamentals

Understand what scenario planning is, when to use it, and how a structured what-if process leads to better planning decisions.

  1. 01

    What is Scenario Planning?

    Scenario planning is the discipline of exploring alternative futures before committing to a course of action. In o9, it means running parallel plans.

    4 min
  2. 02

    When to Use Scenario Planning

    Not every planning decision warrants a scenario. Knowing the triggers — demand uncertainty, supply risk, new launches — helps you apply the practice where it adds the most value.

    3 min
  3. 03

    Types of Scenarios in Supply Chain

    Demand scenarios, supply scenarios, and constraint scenarios serve different questions. Understanding the types helps you frame the right what-if.

    3 min
  4. 04

    The Scenario Planning Process

    A good scenario planning process has four steps: define the question, model the assumption, compare the outputs, and decide. Skipping steps creates confusion.

    4 min
  5. 05

    Setting Up Effective Scenarios

    The quality of a scenario depends on how it is framed. Good scenarios are specific, comparable, and bounded.

    3 min
  6. 06

    Scenarios for Supply Risks

    Supply-side disruptions — supplier failures, capacity shortfalls, logistics delays — require specific scenario types that model constrained supply and test your planning resilience.

    4 min
  7. 07

    Scenarios for Demand Uncertainty

    Demand uncertainty — upside spikes, downside misses, customer concentration risk — requires scenarios that stress-test your supply plan against plausible demand alternatives.

    4 min
