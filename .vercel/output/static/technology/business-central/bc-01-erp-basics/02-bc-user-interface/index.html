The Business Central Interface

The BC web client is organised around role centres, pages, and lists. Understanding the interface structure helps you navigate efficiently and find what you need.

Role centres

When you log into BC, the first thing you see is your role centre — a dashboard configured for your role. A warehouse operator sees a different role centre than a purchase manager. Role centres show the most relevant KPIs, shortcut links, and activity feeds for your job function.

Planners typically work with the Planning or Manufacturing role centre, which surfaces:

  • Open purchase orders and their status
  • Production order queue
  • Item availability by location
  • Demand forecasts (if loaded)

The navigation bar

The top navigation bar is consistent across role centres. It gives access to:

  • Departments — the full menu of all BC modules (Finance, Sales, Purchasing, Manufacturing, Warehouse)
  • Search — type any page, report, or item name to jump directly to it (the fastest navigation method)
  • Settings — user preferences, company selection, connected apps

Pages and lists

Almost everything in BC is either a list page (showing multiple records: all purchase orders, all items) or a card page (showing a single record in detail: one purchase order, one item card). Lists can be filtered, sorted, and exported. From a list, clicking a record opens its card.

Key pages planners use frequently:

  • Items — item master with availability, on-hand, and reorder parameters
  • Purchase Orders — all active purchase orders, their status, and expected receipt dates
  • Production Orders — all production jobs, by status (planned, firm planned, released, finished)
  • Inventory — by location, showing current stock levels

FactBoxes

On card pages, a panel on the right side (the FactBox) shows related information: for an item card, the FactBox might show on-hand by location, open purchase orders, or linked documents. FactBoxes are contextual — they update as you move between records.

Actions and posting

Most transactions in BC are performed through actions — buttons or menu items that appear in the action bar at the top of a page. Common actions: Release, Post, Receive, Ship. Posting is the point at which a transaction becomes permanent in the ledger — once posted, it cannot be undone (only corrected with a reversal entry).
