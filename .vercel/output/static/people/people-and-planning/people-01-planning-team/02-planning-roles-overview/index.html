Planning Roles Overview

A quick-reference guide to the six key roles in a supply chain planning team and what each one does.

S&OP Lead

Owns the end-to-end S&OP cycle. Facilitates cross-functional alignment, drives consensus, and holds accountability for the integrated business plan.

Demand Planner

Generates the statistical baseline forecast, incorporates market intelligence, and manages exceptions through the demand review process.

Supply Planner

Translates the demand plan into a feasible supply response, balancing capacity constraints, inventory targets, and supplier lead times.

Planning Analyst

Maintains data quality, builds reporting, and supports both demand and supply planning with data analysis and root-cause investigation.

Finance Business Partner

Connects the volume plan to the financial plan. Validates that supply chain decisions are consistent with budget and P&L targets.

Commercial Lead

Brings the voice of the market into the demand review: customer commitments, promotional plans, new product launches, and sales assumptions.
