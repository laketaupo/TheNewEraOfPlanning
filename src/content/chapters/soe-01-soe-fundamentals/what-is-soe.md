## The problem S&OE solves

The S&OP cycle sets a plan once a month. But the world changes week by week: demand spikes, a supplier misses a delivery, a quality hold locks a batch, a key customer accelerates an order. The monthly plan cannot respond fast enough on its own — and the next S&OP review is still three weeks away.

Sales & Operations Execution (S&OE) is the weekly process that manages these deviations. It keeps supply aligned with demand at a tactical level, absorbs the disruptions that would otherwise surface as crises, and ensures that execution teams are working to a current and feasible plan.

## How S&OE relates to S&OP

S&OP and S&OE are not competing processes — they operate on different time horizons and at different levels of aggregation:

| | S&OP | S&OE |
|---|---|---|
| **Cadence** | Monthly | Weekly |
| **Horizon** | 3–18 months | 0–12 weeks |
| **Level** | Product family / region | SKU / location |
| **Purpose** | Set the plan | Execute the plan |
| **Focus** | Strategic trade-offs | Tactical adjustments |

S&OE operates within the guardrails set by S&OP. It does not change the monthly plan — it manages execution against it. If a deviation is large enough to require a plan revision, that revision is escalated back to S&OP.

## What S&OE manages

- **Short-term demand deviations** — orders running above or below the weekly plan
- **Supply disruptions** — missed deliveries, quality failures, supplier delays
- **Inventory imbalances** — locations running low or accumulating excess
- **Capacity conflicts** — production schedule clashes that need resolution within the week
- **Exception-driven decisions** — alerts that require human judgment to resolve

## The output of S&OE

The S&OE cycle produces updated short-term plans: replenishment orders confirmed, production schedules adjusted, allocation decisions made. Unlike S&OP, S&OE decisions are operational — they change what happens this week and next, not what the business intends to do in month six.

## Where S&OE sits in the planning hierarchy

S&OE is the bridge between strategic planning (S&OP) and daily execution (order management, production scheduling). It ensures that the work being done today is consistent with the plan agreed last month, and it escalates upward when that is no longer achievable.
