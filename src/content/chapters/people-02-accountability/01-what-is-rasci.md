## The Five Letters

RASCI is a way to make accountability explicit. Every step in a process gets mapped against the people involved, and each person is assigned one of five roles:

- **Responsible** — the person who does the work to complete the step.
- **Accountable** — the single person who owns the outcome and signs it off.
- **Supporting** — people who actively help the Responsible party get the work done.
- **Consulted** — people whose input is sought before the step is finished; this is two-way dialogue.
- **Informed** — people who are told the outcome after the fact; this is one-way communication.

## R Versus A

The distinction that trips teams up most is the difference between Responsible and Accountable. Responsible is the *doer* — the hands on the keyboard, the person building the forecast or running the capacity check. Accountable is the *decision authority* — the person who answers for whether the step was done well and on time.

There can be several people Responsible for a step, but there should only ever be one person Accountable. If two people are Accountable, nobody is, because each can point at the other when something slips.

## Why Planning Teams Need This

Planning is a chain of handoffs across functions: demand passes a forecast to supply, supply passes a plan to finance, finance reconciles it for the executive review. Each handoff is a place where ownership can quietly evaporate. When accountability is implicit, steps get skipped, decisions stall waiting for someone to make a call, and the same arguments resurface every cycle.

A RASCI matrix removes the ambiguity. It states, in advance, exactly who does the work, who decides, who is asked, and who is simply kept in the loop.

## How to Read the Matrix

A RASCI matrix is a grid. Process steps run down the left as rows, and the roles or people run across the top as columns. Each cell holds a letter — or is left blank if that person has no part in that step. To understand any step, read across its row: the single A tells you who owns it, the R (or Rs) tells you who executes, and the C and I cells tell you who is consulted and who is merely informed.
