summary: "BOM Lead Time is the total duration of a transformation process — the time from when production starts to when the output item is available for use or shipment. The planning engine uses lead time to offset supply signals backwards from a due date: if a product is needed on day 10 and takes 3 days to make, production must start on day 7. Accurate lead times are critical for generating feasible plans that don't promise what can't be delivered."
bullets:

  - "Drives backward scheduling — when Planning software must start production"
  - "Typically expressed in hours or days depending on the process"
  - "Can include queue time, setup time, and run time components"
  - "Longer lead times require earlier supply signals to avoid stockouts"
