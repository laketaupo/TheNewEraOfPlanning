## Overview

The tactical planner bridges the gap between strategic intent and operational reality. Their month is anchored by two rhythms running in parallel: the weekly S&OE cycle (monitoring, exceptions, integrated review) and the monthly handover into S&OP (feeding actuals, flagging risks, aligning the near-term plan with the longer-term picture). Their job is to keep the near-term plan credible and executable while the monthly plan is being shaped above them.

---

## Week 1 — S&OE Baseline and Month Open

**S&OE Rhythm: First weekly cycle of the month**
- Run the standard S&OE cycle (demand monitoring → supply monitoring → exception management → integrated review)
- Carry over any unresolved exceptions from the prior month close

**Month Open Activities**
- Review the new monthly plan published by the strategic planner from last month's S&OP
- Disaggregate the monthly plan into weekly buckets; validate against known supply constraints
- Identify immediate gaps between the S&OP plan and current near-term reality
- Flag gaps to the strategic planner — not to re-open S&OP, but to pre-agree the resolution approach

**Key systems:** Planning Software (monthly plan download, weekly disaggregation), ERP (open order position)

---

## Week 2 — Stability and Exception Management

**S&OE Rhythm: Second weekly cycle**
- Continue the S&OE cadence; by mid-month, the near-term plan should be stabilising
- Focus exception effort on items likely to miss the month-end commitment

**Governance Activities**
- Produce the mid-month near-term KPI snapshot for the planning manager
- Review allocation rule effectiveness — are priorities being executed as intended?
- Check data quality: are actuals being posted on time in ERP?

**Key systems:** Planning Software (exception queue, KPI view), ERP (actuals completeness check)

---

## Week 3 — Pre-S&OP Input Preparation

**S&OE Rhythm: Third weekly cycle**
- Continue standard S&OE; attention begins to shift toward month-end close

**S&OP Input Activities**
- Assemble the near-term performance summary for the upcoming S&OP cycle:
  - What moved as planned vs. what did not, and why
  - Demand variance from forecast: structural or one-off?
  - Supply variance from plan: structural constraints or execution failures?
  - Open risks that will carry into the next planning horizon
- Submit the near-term actuals and risk log to the strategic planner as input to the demand and supply S&OP reviews

**Key systems:** Planning Software (actuals vs. plan report), ERP (variance data), reporting tool

---

## Week 4 — Month Close and S&OP Handover

**S&OE Rhythm: Final weekly cycle + month close**
- Final exception resolution push — close out what can be closed before month end
- Confirm all actuals are posted in ERP; escalate any posting delays to data owners
- Run the final integrated S&OE review of the month; document outcomes

**S&OP Handover**
- Present the near-term actuals and open risk log in the pre-S&OP or demand/supply review
- Provide the tactical planner's view on what the next month's near-term plan needs to reflect (constraint flags, known demand events, supplier risks)
- Hand off to the strategic planner for the S&OP cycle to run

**Key systems:** ERP (month-end actuals confirmation), Planning Software (final near-term plan), S&OP reporting pack

---

## The Tactical Planner's Monthly Rhythm at a Glance

| Week | S&OE Focus | S&OP / Broader Focus |
|---|---|---|
| Week 1 | Cycle 1: baseline reset | Receive new monthly plan; disaggregate and validate |
| Week 2 | Cycle 2: stability | Mid-month KPIs; allocation and data quality checks |
| Week 3 | Cycle 3: risk focus | Assemble near-term actuals and risk log for S&OP input |
| Week 4 | Cycle 4: close out | Month-end close; S&OP handover and risk flag |

---

## How the Tactical Planner Connects to the S&OP Cycle

The tactical planner does not run S&OP — that is the strategic planner's domain. But the tactical planner is the primary source of near-term truth that feeds into S&OP. Without accurate actuals, a well-curated exception log, and a clear view of near-term constraints, the S&OP process starts from a flawed baseline.

The handover between tactical and strategic planning is not a one-time monthly event — it is a continuous information flow that makes the two rhythms coherent.
