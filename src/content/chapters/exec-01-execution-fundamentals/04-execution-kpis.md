## Why execution KPIs are distinct

Execution KPIs measure what actually happened, not what was planned. They are the feedback signal that tells the organisation whether the planning process is translating into real-world outcomes. A perfect S&OP plan that consistently fails in execution is still a failing supply chain.

Execution KPIs are also the early warning system: a deteriorating fill rate or rising OTIF miss rate signals a problem before it becomes a customer complaint or a financial miss.

## Key execution KPIs

**Order fill rate**
The percentage of customer orders fulfilled completely from available stock at the time of order. Fill rate measures whether the inventory position is adequate to meet demand without backorders or substitutions.

**On-time in-full (OTIF)**
The percentage of orders delivered on the date committed, with the full quantity ordered. OTIF is the most customer-facing execution KPI — it directly reflects the service level experienced by the customer.

**Production schedule adherence**
The percentage of the confirmed production schedule that was actually completed on time. Adherence below target indicates capacity constraints, unplanned downtime, or material shortages that are causing the plan to slip.

**Inbound receipt accuracy**
The percentage of purchase orders received on time and in full from suppliers. Receipt accuracy is the upstream counterpart to OTIF — if inbound is unreliable, outbound service will follow.

**Inventory record accuracy (IRA)**
The percentage of inventory locations where the physical count matches the system record. IRA below 98–99% creates systemic planning errors: the plan reserves stock that is not there, and available stock goes unallocated.

**Order-to-ship cycle time**
The elapsed time between order confirmation and shipment. Shortening cycle time increases responsiveness; unexpected lengthening signals process breakdown in the warehouse or at the carrier interface.

## Connecting execution KPIs to planning

Execution KPIs should feed back into the S&OE process. A persistent fill rate miss is an S&OE exception. A structural OTIF problem is an S&OP escalation. The planning process only improves if it is informed by what is actually happening in execution — not just what the plan predicted.
