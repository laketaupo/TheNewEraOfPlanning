## The purpose of pre-S&OP

Pre-S&OP is a working session — not a presentation. Demand and supply leads, finance, and the S&OP process owner meet to work through the gap list produced by the supply review. Items that can be resolved without executive escalation are resolved here. Items that require trade-offs beyond operational authority are packaged as structured decision options for the executive review.

A well-run pre-S&OP means the executive meeting is short: no surprises, no data debates, just decisions on pre-prepared options.

## What gets resolved in pre-S&OP

- **Operational gaps** — shortfalls that can be closed through inventory draw-down, overtime, or supplier call-offs within existing authority
- **Demand adjustments** — cases where a commercial overlay looks inconsistent with supply reality and the teams agree to revise it
- **Minor trade-offs** — service level misses on low-priority SKUs that operations can make without executive approval
- **Data issues** — inconsistencies between the demand and supply models that need reconciliation before the numbers go to leadership

## What goes to the executive review

- **Material supply shortfalls** — gaps that require significant capital, supplier contract changes, or decisions that affect service level commitments to major customers
- **Revenue vs. cost trade-offs** — e.g., build ahead at significant inventory carrying cost vs. accept a potential service level miss
- **Capacity investment decisions** — requests for capital expenditure or long-term supplier commitments
- **Cross-functional disagreements** — where demand and supply cannot agree and need a senior decision

## The decision package

For each item going to the executive review, pre-S&OP prepares a structured decision package:

1. **What is the gap** — volume, value, timing, affected customers
2. **Options** — typically 2–3 costed options, with pros and cons
3. **Recommendation** — the cross-functional team's preferred option with reasoning
4. **Decision required** — what the executive needs to approve

The executive meeting is not the place to discover the problem for the first time. Pre-S&OP exists precisely to prevent that.

## Signs pre-S&OP is not working

- The executive review routinely runs long because basic data debates happen in the room
- Items escalated to executives could have been resolved at a lower level
- The same issues appear on the escalation list month after month
- Decisions made in the executive review are reversed within days by operational teams
