## The core purpose of S&OP

Sales & Operations Planning (S&OP) is a structured monthly process that brings together demand, supply, inventory, and financial plans into one agreed-upon view of the business. Its purpose is alignment: ensuring that sales commitments are backed by supply capacity, that financial targets are grounded in operational reality, and that cross-functional decisions are made together rather than in silos.

S&OP operates at an **aggregate level** — typically by product family, region, and month. It is not a day-to-day execution process. It is a planning process that sets the constraints and priorities within which execution happens.

## The S&OP cycle

A standard S&OP cycle runs monthly and has four to five steps:

1. **Demand review** — the commercial team presents the updated demand forecast. Differences from the prior forecast are explained and challenged. The output is a consensus demand plan.

2. **Supply review** — operations reviews whether supply capacity can meet the consensus demand. Gaps are identified: where is supply short? Where is there excess capacity?

3. **Pre-S&OP (or integrated reconciliation)** — demand and supply teams meet to resolve gaps before the executive meeting. Trade-off options are prepared: can we build ahead? Accept a service level miss? Adjust the demand plan?

4. **Executive S&OP** — leadership reviews unresolved gaps, approves trade-offs, and signs off on the plan. Decisions made here become binding for the business.

5. **Plan publication** — the agreed plan is communicated to execution teams and loaded into operational systems (ERP, scheduling, procurement).

## What S&OP is not

S&OP is not a forecasting meeting. The forecast is an input, not the output. S&OP is also not an operations review — it does not manage last week's shipments or resolve today's quality issue. Those belong in S&OE.

Confusing the two is a common failure mode: executive meetings get dragged into day-to-day firefighting, and the long-term plan never gets the structured attention it needs.

## S&OP in an agricultural context

For seed and crop planning businesses, S&OP has to account for long lead times and seasonal production cycles. A decision made in the executive S&OP in October may determine what gets planted the following spring. This makes the quality of decision-making in S&OP especially consequential — and makes the connection between the S&OP plan and the Planning software planning model critical.
