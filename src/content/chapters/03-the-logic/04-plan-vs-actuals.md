## The Plan–Actual Loop

No plan survives contact with reality unchanged. The value of o9 is not just in generating the plan — it's in continuously comparing the plan to actuals and responding to deviations **before they escalate**.

```
Plan generated
      │
      ▼
Execution begins (orders placed, production starts)
      │
      ▼
Actuals flow in (shipments confirmed, production reported)
      │
      ▼
o9 compares plan vs. actuals
      │
      ▼
Deviations surface as exceptions
      │
      ▼
Planner reviews, decides, re-plans
```

## What Gets Compared

| Plan signal | Actual signal | Deviation type |
|---|---|---|
| Planned production qty | Reported production qty | Under/over-production |
| Planned receipt date | Actual goods receipt date | Late/early delivery |
| Forecast demand | Actual sales orders | Demand deviation |
| Planned inventory | Physical inventory count | Stock discrepancy |

## S&OE: The Weekly Execution Review

The **Sales & Operations Execution (S&OE)** process is where plan-vs-actual analysis happens on a weekly (or even daily) basis. In o9, the S&OE workbench shows:

- Items at risk of stockout in the next 4 weeks
- Production orders behind schedule
- Supplier POs with confirmed delays
- Customer orders at risk of late shipment
