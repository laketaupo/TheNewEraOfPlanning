## Follow the Planning Cycle

This walkthrough guides you through a complete planning cycle in o9 — from opening your workbench to releasing supply orders. Click **Next** to advance through each step.

<!-- Widget rendered by the page layout when widget = "walkthrough" -->

## The Steps in Brief

1. **Open the workbench** — navigate to your planning view (demand, supply, or integrated)
2. **Review exceptions** — see what has changed since the last planning cycle
3. **Assess demand** — confirm or adjust the demand forecast for the planning horizon
4. **Run the supply plan** — trigger the planning engine (constrained or unconstrained)
5. **Review capacity** — check for overloads and underloads on critical resources
6. **Resolve conflicts** — accept AI suggestions or manually adjust orders
7. **Commit the plan** — lock the baseline for the current review cycle
8. **Release orders** — push planned production orders and POs to execution systems

## Key Questions at Each Step

| Step | Question to answer |
|---|---|
| Exceptions review | What changed and does it need action? |
| Demand review | Is the forecast aligned with market intelligence? |
| Supply run | Is the plan feasible within current constraints? |
| Capacity review | Where is the binding constraint this cycle? |
| Conflict resolution | What is the lowest-cost way to close the gap? |
| Order release | Are planners aligned and approvals obtained? |
