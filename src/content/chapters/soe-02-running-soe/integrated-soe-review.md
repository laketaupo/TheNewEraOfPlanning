## The weekly alignment point

The integrated S&OE review is the weekly equivalent of the pre-S&OP in the monthly cycle. Demand and supply planners, supported by the S&OE coordinator, meet to align on the near-term plan: what are the open exceptions, what has been resolved, what requires a cross-functional decision, and what needs to be escalated to S&OP.

This meeting typically runs 60–90 minutes. It is not a status update. It is a decision forum for the items that individual planners could not resolve on their own.

## Agenda structure

**1. Exception queue review (15 min)**
How many exceptions are open? What is the priority breakdown? Which ones have been resolved since the last review?

**2. Supply-constrained items (20–30 min)**
Items where supply cannot meet near-term demand. For each: what is the root cause, what options are available, what is the recommendation, and who needs to decide?

**3. Demand deviations (15–20 min)**
Items where actual orders are running significantly above or below the near-term plan. Are these one-off deviations or signals of a structural change?

**4. Escalation list (10–15 min)**
Items that cannot be resolved within the S&OE process. Each item should be documented with context and a clear articulation of what decision is needed from S&OP.

**5. Plan confirmation (5 min)**
Confirm that the near-term plan — production schedule, replenishment orders, allocation decisions — is agreed and ready to publish to execution.

## Who facilitates

The S&OE coordinator or IBP manager facilitates the review. Their role is to maintain the agenda, prevent the discussion from diving into operational detail that belongs at the planner level, and ensure each item ends with a documented decision or action.

## Common failure modes

**Too long** — a 3-hour weekly integrated review is a sign that decisions are being made that should have been resolved at the individual planner level, or that the exception queue is too large and needs better management.

**Missing participation** — if the supply review goes ahead without operations representation, the decisions made are not grounded in reality. The integrated review requires both demand and supply to be present.

**No escalation log** — items flagged for S&OP escalation that are not documented are items that will be raised verbally in a future meeting with no context. The escalation log is the institutional memory of the S&OE process.
