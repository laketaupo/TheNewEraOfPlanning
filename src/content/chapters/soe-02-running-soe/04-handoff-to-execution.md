## The boundary between S&OE and execution

S&OE manages the near-term plan — typically weeks 1–6. Execution manages what happens within the current operating day or shift. The handoff between S&OE and execution is the point at which the plan transitions from something that can still be adjusted to something that is being acted on.

A clean handoff means execution teams receive:
- A confirmed production schedule for the near-term period
- Released replenishment and purchase orders
- Allocation decisions for constrained inventory
- Any special instructions arising from exceptions resolved in S&OE

## Plan publication in planning software

Planning software formalises the handoff through plan publication: a step in the system that locks the near-term plan and releases orders to ERP or execution systems. Once published, changes require a deliberate override — preventing ad-hoc plan revisions by individuals outside the S&OE process.

The publication step also triggers downstream actions: warehouse management systems receive pick and put-away tasks, transportation management systems receive shipment confirmations, and manufacturing execution systems receive the production schedule.

## What execution teams need from S&OE

Execution teams need clarity, not ambiguity. A handoff that works well gives execution:

- **A feasible plan** — not one that requires constant re-planning within the day
- **Clear priorities** — when constraints exist, which orders or customers take precedence
- **Known exceptions** — any supply risks, quality holds, or availability constraints that execution needs to be aware of
- **A contact point** — who to call if something unexpected happens that requires a plan change within the day

## When execution finds a problem

Execution teams will sometimes encounter issues that were not visible in S&OE: a machinery breakdown, a transport failure, a customer rejection. These need to feed back into S&OE quickly. A feedback channel — whether a direct call to the planner on duty, a system-generated exception, or a daily operational call — is essential to keeping S&OE informed of what is actually happening on the ground.

## What comes next

S&OE hands off a feasible plan. Execution's job is to deliver it — and to keep the planning model honest when reality diverges from the plan.

The Execution Process module covers the daily layer: how execution teams prioritise orders under constraint, maintain real-time visibility, enforce execution discipline, and feed actuals back to planning so the S&OE and S&OP models stay current.
